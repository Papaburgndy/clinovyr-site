Which Is Right for You?

LASIK is best for most patients seeking fast visual recovery. PRK is ideal for thin-cornea candidates and those avoiding flap risks.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long until I see clearly?
LASIK: 24 hours for most, 1 week for full vision. PRK: 3-5 days for functional, 1-2 weeks for full clarity.
Which is safer?
Both are safe. LASIK has faster recovery; PRK has fewer flap-related risks. Success rates are comparable.
Can I play sports after surgery?
LASIK: 1-2 weeks. PRK: 3-4 weeks due to the corneal scraping and longer healing.
Which should I choose?
Choose LASIK for fast recovery and comfort. Choose PRK if you have thin corneas or want to avoid flap risks.
