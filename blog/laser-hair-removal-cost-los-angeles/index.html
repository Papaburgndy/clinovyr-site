Laser Hair Removal Prices by LA Neighborhood

Where you get treated in LA affects pricing significantly. Beverly Hills and West Hollywood clinics charge a premium for the same technology available at lower prices in other neighborhoods.

Beverly Hills & West Hollywood

Expect to pay 20–40% above city averages. Full Brazilian sessions run $350–$500. These clinics cater to a luxury clientele and often use the latest multi-wavelength platforms. Board-certified dermatologists and plastic surgeons predominate. Best for: patients wanting top-tier providers and willing to pay for prestige.

Santa Monica & Westside

Slightly below Beverly Hills pricing — Brazilian sessions $275–$400. Strong concentration of experienced dermatology practices and med spas. Good balance of quality and value. Many clinics near the beach have built reputations on body treatments including laser hair removal.

Hollywood & Mid-Wilshire

Competitive pricing with a wide range of providers. Brazilian sessions $225–$375. Mix of established dermatology offices and newer med spas. Check credentials carefully — provider quality varies more in this area than in Beverly Hills or Santa Monica.

Downtown LA & Koreatown

Some of the most competitive pricing in the city. Brazilian sessions $200–$325. Koreatown in particular has excellent aesthetics clinics drawing from a strong beauty culture. Many K-beauty-influenced clinics offer advanced technology at lower price points.

San Fernando Valley (Sherman Oaks, Encino, Studio City)

Generally 15–25% below Westside pricing. Brazilian sessions $200–$350. Several well-established dermatology and laser clinics serve the Valley. Lower overhead translates to meaningful savings without sacrificing quality.

South Bay (Manhattan Beach, Hermosa, Torrance)

Mid-range pricing similar to the Valley. Brazilian sessions $225–$375. Growing number of specialized laser clinics. Good option for South Bay residents who want to avoid the drive to West LA or Beverly Hills.

Brazilian Laser Hair Removal in LA: Complete Guide

Brazilian laser hair removal is the most-requested treatment in Los Angeles. Here's what to know about pricing, preparation, and what to expect.

What's Included

A full Brazilian treatment covers the entire pubic area — front, sides, labia, and perianal area. Some clinics differentiate between "bikini line" (just the edges visible in swimwear) and "full Brazilian" (everything). Always confirm what's included before booking.

Typical Treatment Timeline

Sessions are spaced 4–6 weeks apart. Most patients see significant reduction after 3–4 sessions and reach 80–90% permanent reduction after 6–8 sessions. A full treatment course takes approximately 6–10 months from start to finish. Maintenance sessions every 6–12 months may be needed, especially for hormonal regrowth.

Preparation Tips

  • Shave 24 hours before your appointment (do not wax or pluck — the laser needs the hair root intact)
  • Avoid sun exposure for 2 weeks before treatment to minimize hyperpigmentation risk
  • Skip retinoids and chemical exfoliants for 5–7 days before treatment
  • Request numbing cream if it's your first session — most clinics apply it 20–30 minutes before treatment
  • Wear loose clothing to your appointment for comfort afterward

How to Choose a Laser Hair Removal Provider in LA

✅ Provider Checklist — 5 Things to Verify
  • Board certification: Look for dermatologists (AAD) or plastic surgeons (ABPS). Med spa NPs and PAs should be supervised by a licensed physician.
  • Laser technology: Ask specifically what device they use. Avoid clinics that can't name their laser or only offer IPL for hair removal.
  • Skin tone assessment: A quality provider will assess your Fitzpatrick skin type and choose the appropriate laser. Walk away if they use the same laser on everyone.
  • Before/after photos: Request photos of patients with similar skin tones and body areas to yours.
  • Transparent pricing: Get total package costs in writing, including any potential touch-up sessions. Avoid clinics that won't quote prices until you're in-office.
⚠️ Red flags to avoid: Prices dramatically below market ($50/session full Brazilian), Groupon-only businesses with no standalone web presence, unlicensed technicians performing treatments without physician supervision, and clinics that pressure you into signing a contract at your first consultation.

Deals, Packages & Financing Options

Package Discounts

Most LA clinics offer tiered pricing: single sessions at full price, packages of 4 at 10–15% off, and packages of 6–8 at 20–25% off. Some include free maintenance sessions with larger packages. Multi-area bundles (e.g., Brazilian + underarms + lower legs) can save 25–35% versus booking each area separately.

Seasonal Promotions

The best deals typically appear in January/February (New Year promotions) and September/October (off-season before holiday parties). Summer is peak demand — expect fewer discounts from May through August.

Financing & Payment Plans

CareCredit, Cherry, and PatientFi are widely accepted at LA laser clinics. Many offer 0% interest for 6–12 months on treatments over $1,000. Some clinics have in-house membership programs offering 10–20% off all treatments year-round.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does laser hair removal cost in Los Angeles?

LA laser hair removal costs $75–$500 per session depending on body area. Small areas like the upper lip start at $75–$150. Medium areas like the bikini line range $150–$275. Large areas like full legs cost $350–$500. Packages of 6 sessions save 20–25% versus single sessions.

How much does Brazilian laser hair removal cost in LA?

A single Brazilian laser session in Los Angeles costs $250–$450. Most clinics offer packages of 6 sessions for $1,200–$2,400. Beverly Hills clinics sit at the higher end; Valley and Downtown clinics offer more competitive rates. Always confirm what "Brazilian" includes — full front-to-back coverage versus bikini line only.

Is laser hair removal permanent?

Laser hair removal provides permanent hair reduction of 80–90% after a full course of 6–8 treatments. Some maintenance sessions may be needed every 12–18 months, particularly for hormonally-influenced areas (face, bikini). Results depend on hair color, skin tone, and the laser technology used.

What type of laser is best for hair removal?

The best laser depends on your skin tone. Alexandrite lasers (755nm) work best for light skin. Nd:YAG lasers (1064nm) are safest for dark skin. Diode lasers work across a range of tones. Soprano ICE platforms combine multiple wavelengths. Always ask your provider what device they use and why it's appropriate for your skin type.

How many sessions do I need?

Most people need 6–8 sessions spaced 4–6 weeks apart. Coarse, dark hair (bikini, underarms) often responds in 6 sessions. Finer hair or hormonal areas may need 8–10. Your provider should give you a realistic session estimate during your consultation — be skeptical of anyone who promises results in 3–4 sessions.

Does laser hair removal hurt?

Pain varies by technology and body area. Older lasers feel like a rubber band snap. Newer platforms like Soprano ICE use gradual heating and are nearly painless. Sensitive areas (bikini, upper lip) hurt more than legs or arms. Most clinics offer topical numbing cream for sensitive areas — request it at booking.

Is laser hair removal safe for dark skin?

Yes, but only with the right laser. Nd:YAG (1064nm) lasers and Soprano-type platforms are safe for Fitzpatrick IV–VI skin tones. Alexandrite and IPL devices can burn darker skin and cause lasting hyperpigmentation. This is the most critical safety factor — always confirm the laser type before treatment.

How do I prepare for laser hair removal?

Shave the treatment area 24 hours before (never wax or pluck). Avoid sun exposure for 2 weeks prior. Stop retinoids and exfoliants 5–7 days before. Wear loose, comfortable clothing. Arrive with clean skin — no lotions, deodorant, or makeup on the treatment area.
