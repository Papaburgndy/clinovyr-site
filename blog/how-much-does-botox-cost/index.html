How Much Does Botox Cost in 2026? (City-by-City Price Guide)

By Clinovyr Editorial Team · 2026-03-08 · 9 min read

Botox Pricing: What You're Really Paying For

Botox pricing in the United States varies more than most people realize. The same treatment — say, forehead lines — can cost $200 in a medical spa in suburban Texas and $900 at a boutique clinic in Manhattan. Understanding how pricing works helps you find legitimate value without sacrificing safety.

How Botox Is Priced

Botox is sold in two ways: per unit or per area. Understanding the difference is critical to comparing prices fairly.

  • Per unit pricing: Each unit of Botox toxin has a set cost, typically $10–$20 per unit. You pay for exactly how many units are injected. More transparent and easier to compare.
  • Per area pricing: You pay a flat fee for treating a specific area (e.g., forehead = $250). Can be better value if you need more units, worse if you need fewer.

Typical unit counts by treatment area:

  • Forehead lines: 10–30 units ($100–$600)
  • Frown lines (glabellar/11s): 15–25 units ($150–$500)
  • Crow's feet: 5–15 units per side ($100–$300 total)
  • Lip flip: 4–6 units ($40–$120)
  • Brow lift: 4–8 units ($40–$160)
  • Full face (3 areas): 40–70 units ($400–$1,400)

Why Prices Vary So Much

The biggest factors affecting Botox price:

  • Provider credentials: Board-certified plastic surgeons and dermatologists charge more than RNs or PAs at medical spas. Both can inject safely — the premium for a surgeon is about their experience with complex cases, not basic cosmetic Botox.
  • Brand of neurotoxin: Botox (Allergan) is typically priced higher than Dysport, Jeuveau, or Xeomin — which all work similarly. Dysport often requires slightly more units but costs less per unit.
  • Location overhead: A clinic in Beverly Hills has dramatically higher rent than one in suburban Phoenix. That's passed to patients.
  • Loyalty programs: Allergan's Allē program gives points on Botox purchases you can redeem for discounts. Evolus (Jeuveau) has a similar program. Can meaningfully reduce ongoing costs.

Red Flags: When Cheap Botox Is Dangerous

Never choose a Botox provider based on the lowest price alone. Warning signs of unsafe providers:

  • Prices dramatically below market (e.g., $5/unit or $99 "full face" specials)
  • No medical director listed on their website
  • Injections performed in non-medical settings (nail salons, parking lots — yes, this happens)
  • No consultation before injection
  • Unable to tell you which brand of neurotoxin they're using

Counterfeit Botox exists and has sent people to the hospital. Verify providers are licensed and reputable before booking.
