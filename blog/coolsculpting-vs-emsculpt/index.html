CoolSculpting vs Emsculpt: Which Body Contouring Treatment Is Better?

By Clinovyr Editorial Team · 2026-02-26 · 8 min read

Two Very Different Approaches to Body Contouring

CoolSculpting and Emsculpt are both FDA-cleared body contouring devices offered at med spas across the country. Both are non-surgical. But they work completely differently, target different problems, and deliver very different results. Understanding the distinction helps you avoid spending $3,000+ on the wrong treatment.

CoolSculpting: Fat Elimination

CoolSculpting uses cryolipolysis — controlled cooling — to freeze and kill fat cells. The frozen cells undergo apoptosis (programmed cell death) and are gradually eliminated by your immune system over 1–3 months. It's FDA-cleared and genuinely effective at reducing fat in specific, stubborn pockets that don't respond well to diet and exercise.

What CoolSculpting Does Well

  • Reduces fat in targeted areas by 20–25% per treatment cycle
  • Best for: love handles, lower belly, inner/outer thighs, bra fat, double chin
  • Requires no downtime (return to work same day)
  • Results visible in 1–3 months after treatment

CoolSculpting Costs

  • Single applicator cycle: $600–$1,000
  • Full abdomen treatment (2–4 cycles): $1,500–$4,000
  • Multiple areas full session: $3,000–$8,000+

Note: CoolSculpting's "Elite" dual applicator system can treat two areas simultaneously, reducing time but maintaining similar pricing.

Emsculpt NEO: Muscle Building + Fat Burning

Emsculpt NEO combines HIFEM (high-intensity focused electromagnetic energy) with RF (radiofrequency) heat. The HIFEM energy induces supramaximal muscle contractions — the equivalent of 20,000 sit-ups in 30 minutes. The RF heat simultaneously destroys fat cells. The result: both fat reduction AND muscle building in the same treatment.

What Emsculpt NEO Does Well

  • Reduces fat by ~30% AND increases muscle mass by ~25% in treated area
  • Best for: abdomen, buttocks, arms, calves, thighs
  • No downtime, but expect soreness like a hard workout
  • Results visible after 4 sessions over 2–4 weeks; peak results at 3 months

Emsculpt NEO Costs

  • Single session: $750–$1,000
  • Recommended package (4 sessions): $2,500–$4,000

Which Should You Choose?

Choose CoolSculpting if: You have a specific stubborn fat deposit (love handles, lower belly pouch) that hasn't responded to diet and exercise. You're at or near your goal weight and want targeted fat reduction. You don't need muscle building.

Choose Emsculpt NEO if: You want both fat reduction AND muscle definition. You want to improve athletic appearance or lift your glutes without surgery. You're willing to tolerate post-treatment muscle soreness for enhanced results.

Both if your budget allows: Some providers offer combination protocols — CoolSculpting first for fat reduction, then Emsculpt NEO for muscle definition and additional fat burning.
