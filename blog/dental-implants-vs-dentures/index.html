Dental Implants vs Dentures: Cost, Comfort, and Which Is Right for You

By Clinovyr Editorial Team · 2026-02-28 · 9 min read

The Real Cost of Replacing Missing Teeth

Missing teeth affect more than your smile — they impact speech, eating, jawbone health, and confidence. The two most common replacement options, dental implants and dentures, differ dramatically in cost, procedure, maintenance, and long-term outcomes. Here's what no one tells you about both options.

Dental Implants: The Permanent Solution

A dental implant is a titanium post surgically placed into the jawbone, topped with an abutment and a crown. It functions and looks exactly like a natural tooth. The procedure involves:

  • Implant placement surgery: 1–2 hour procedure under local anesthesia (or sedation)
  • Osseointegration: 3–6 months for the implant to fuse with the jawbone
  • Crown placement: Final crown attached after integration

Total timeline: 6–18 months depending on bone health and healing.

Dental Implant Costs (2026)

  • Single tooth implant: $3,000–$6,000 (implant + abutment + crown)
  • Multiple teeth: $6,000–$30,000+ depending on number
  • Full arch (All-on-4): $20,000–$50,000 per arch
  • With bone grafting: Add $500–$3,000 per area

Dentures: The Affordable Alternative

Dentures are removable appliances that replace some or all teeth. Traditional dentures rest on the gums; implant-supported dentures ("snap-on dentures") attach to implants for stability. Types include:

  • Full dentures: Replace all teeth on upper, lower, or both arches. $1,500–$5,000 per arch.
  • Partial dentures: Replace some teeth when others remain. $700–$2,500.
  • Implant-supported dentures: Attached to 2–4 implants per arch for security. $6,000–$30,000 per arch.
  • Immediate (same-day) dentures: Placed at extraction. Less precise fit, require adjustment. $1,000–$3,000.
