Best Areas for Med Spas in Austin

Austin's med spa scene is concentrated in a few key areas — each with a distinct character and price point.

🏙️ Domain / North Austin

Highest concentration of luxury med spas in Austin. Premium brands, latest technology, and highest prices. Great for complex procedures like CoolSculpting, laser resurfacing, and RF microneedling.

🌮 South Congress / South Austin

Mid-range boutique spas with a local Austin vibe. Often 10–20% less expensive than Domain. Strong for injectables, HydraFacials, and laser hair removal. Many offer loyalty programs.

🏝️ West Lake Hills / Westlake

Upscale suburban practices serving the Westlake Hills area. Similar pricing to Domain but more appointment availability and easier parking. Popular for Botox and filler maintenance.

🎵 Downtown / East Austin

Growing med spa presence in trendy East Austin. Best for younger patients looking for Botox basics and facials. More competitive pricing with frequent new-patient specials.

5 Red Flags When Choosing an Austin Med Spa

Texas law requires a licensed physician to supervise med spa injections — but enforcement varies. Here's what to watch for.

🚩
No physician medical director on record — Texas requires a licensed MD to supervise all injectable procedures. Ask for the medical director's name and verify their license at the Texas Medical Board website.
🚩
Injectors with no nursing or PA credentials — In Texas, Botox and fillers must be injected by or under the supervision of a licensed NP, PA, or physician. Aestheticians cannot legally inject.
🚩
Prices dramatically below market — Botox under $8/unit in Austin almost always means diluted product, off-brand toxin, or under-credentialed injectors. Bargain Botox is high-risk.
🚩
No before/after consultation — Any reputable Austin med spa will do a facial assessment and show you a treatment plan before injecting anything. Walk out if they want to start immediately.
🚩
No emergency protocol discussed — Good injectors mention vascular occlusion risks and confirm they have hyaluronidase on-site for emergency filler dissolution. If they seem unfamiliar with complications, leave.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about med spas in Austin.

How much does Botox cost at Austin med spas?
Botox in Austin ranges from $10–$16 per unit at most reputable med spas, compared to $14–$20+ in NYC or LA. A standard forehead treatment uses 10–30 units ($100–$480). Many Austin practices sell Botox by the area ($150–$250 for forehead, $100–$175 for crow's feet) rather than by the unit — always confirm which pricing model applies and exactly how many units you're getting.
What is the best med spa in Austin for first-timers?
First-time med spa patients in Austin should prioritize practices that offer free consultations, have injectors who take time to explain everything, and don't pressure you to buy packages upfront. Look for RN or NP injectors with at least 2–3 years of aesthetic experience. Reviews mentioning "not overdone" or "natural results" are a good sign. Start with a conservative amount of Botox — you can always add more at a follow-up visit 2 weeks later.
Do Austin med spas require a doctor's visit?
Texas law requires physician oversight of injectable procedures, but you do not typically meet with the physician directly — they supervise the practice and review protocols. You'll usually be treated by a Nurse Practitioner (NP) or Physician Assistant (PA) who handles the actual injection. If a practice claims their aesthetician (without nursing license) can inject Botox or fillers, this is illegal in Texas and you should leave.
How long does Botox last in Austin's climate?
Botox typically lasts 3–4 months regardless of climate, though some patients in Austin report slightly faster metabolism in summer due to heat and physical activity. If you're physically active (hiking, hot yoga, cycling — all popular in Austin), your Botox may metabolize slightly faster. Plan for touch-ups every 3–4 months, and consider a slightly higher unit count in active muscle areas like the forehead if you're very athletic.
Are Austin med spas safe?
Yes — when properly licensed and supervised by a physician, med spa procedures in Austin are safe and FDA-approved. The key is verifying credentials: confirm the medical director's Texas Medical Board license, check that your injector is an NP, PA, or RN, and ensure they use brand-name products (Botox, Juvederm, Restylane) not unlicensed alternatives.
